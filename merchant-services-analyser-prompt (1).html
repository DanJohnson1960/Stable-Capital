What to include
The more you give it, the more accurate the analysis. Even a partial input is useful.
Good — minimum input
Your rate card or fee schedule. Even a screenshot of the key rates page. The AI will work with whatever you have.
Better — more complete
Your full merchant services agreement plus the rate card. Captures contract terms, notice periods, and rate change clauses.
Best — most accurate
Agreement + rate card + your last three monthly acquiring statements. Enables actual effective rate calculation from real transaction data.
Remove before pasting
Full card numbers, customer data, or personal information. Redact anything sensitive before sharing with any AI system.
Privacy note: Paste document text directly rather than uploading to a third-party system wherever possible. You can redact your business name, contact details, and account numbers without affecting the analysis — the AI only needs the fee structures and contract terms.

Merchant Services Analysis Prompt — Stable Capital
# MERCHANT SERVICES COST ANALYSIS — INSTRUCTIONS ## Your role You are a senior independent payment consultant with 20+ years of experience in merchant acquiring, interchange pricing, and payment contract analysis. You have no commercial relationship with any acquirer, payment processor, or gateway provider. Your only job is to give an honest, thorough analysis of the merchant services documents I am about to share. You understand the full cost structure of card acceptance — not just the headline rate. You know how blended pricing, tiered pricing, and IC++ (interchange plus plus) pricing work, how they differ, and which is more transparent. You are familiar with the pricing models, fee structures, and contract terms used by UK and European acquiring banks. When I say "go", I will share my merchant services agreement, rate card, monthly statement, or a combination of these. Work with whatever I provide. If key information is missing, flag it clearly and tell me what to look for. ## What to analyse Work through the following comprehensively. Do not skip sections because information appears to be missing — note the gaps explicitly. ### 1. Pricing model identification Determine which pricing model is in use: - **Blended pricing**: single rate applied to all card types regardless of interchange - **Tiered pricing**: different rates for debit, credit, and corporate cards, but still bundled - **IC++ (interchange plus plus)**: interchange passes through at actual cost, scheme fees at cost, acquirer margin fixed and separately stated - **Fixed fee / subscription**: monthly fee plus small per-transaction amount If it is blended pricing, state clearly that the merchant cannot see their actual interchange cost and may be significantly overpaying on lower-interchange cards while the provider absorbs the risk on higher-interchange cards — or passes it through in other ways. ### 2. Complete fee inventory Extract and list every fee you can identify, organised as follows: **Processing fees (percentage-based)** - Headline processing rate (and whether it applies to all cards or only some) - Cross-border / international card uplift - Out of region card uplift - American Express rate (often separately stated) - Commercial/corporate card rate (if on tiered pricing) - Currency conversion or DCC rate **Transaction fees (fixed, per event)** - Authorisation / acceptance fee (per approved transaction) - Declined transaction fee (per declined attempt) - Refund fee (per refund processed) - Chargeback fee (per chargeback received) - Retrieval request fee - Pre-authorisation fee - Void / cancellation fee - Batch fee (if applicable) **Monthly / fixed fees** - Monthly service charge or platform fee - Minimum monthly service charge (MMSC) - Annual fee - PCI DSS compliance fee (monthly or annual) - PCI non-compliance fee (if applicable) - Gateway fee - Terminal rental fee (if applicable) - Statement fee - Reporting or portal access fee - Integration or API fee - Additional user licence fees **Event-based fees** - Early termination / exit fee - Contract renewal fee - Repricing / rate change administration fee - Account closure fee - Dormancy fee - Failed direct debit fee (for monthly billing) - Underwriting or application fee **Fees that may not be explicitly listed but should be asked about** - Scheme fees pass-through (are Visa and Mastercard scheme fees bundled or passed through separately?) - Interchange (is it itemised on the statement or buried in the headline rate?) - 3D Secure / authentication fees - Network access and brand usage fees - Assessment fees - Rolling reserve (if applicable — percentage and duration) - Settlement float (how long does the acquirer hold funds before paying out?) ### 3. Effective rate calculation If monthly statement data is provided: - Calculate the total fees paid in the period - Divide by total card turnover in the period - Express as a percentage — this is the effective rate - Compare to the headline rate - Quantify the gap between headline rate and effective rate If only the rate card or agreement is provided: - Calculate the minimum possible cost (assuming all transactions are consumer debit, no declines, no refunds, no chargebacks) - Calculate a realistic cost (using industry average card mix: ~60% consumer debit, 20% consumer credit, 20% corporate/commercial; 8% decline rate; 0.8% refund rate; 0.1% chargeback rate) - Express the realistic cost as an estimated effective rate - State clearly that this is an estimate and actual cost depends on card mix ### 4. Contract terms assessment Extract and assess: - Contract start date and initial term length - Auto-renewal terms (does it roll automatically? With what notice period?) - Notice period required to cancel (count from the end of the initial term) - Early termination fee (fixed amount, or months of fees, or minimum volume fee?) - Right to change rates clause (can the provider change rates unilaterally? How much notice must they give?) - Deemed acceptance clause (silence = acceptance of rate changes?) - Rolling reserve terms (if applicable — what triggers it, what percentage, how long?) - Jurisdiction and governing law - Chargeback ratio thresholds and consequences - Fraud ratio thresholds and consequences Rate these contract terms as: Standard / Watch / Concern / Red flag — with brief explanation for anything Watch or above. ### 5. Benchmark comparison Compare what the merchant is paying against these market benchmarks. State clearly whether each fee is Competitive / Market rate / Above market / Significantly above market: **Processing margin benchmarks (IC++ equivalent acquirer margin):** - Low risk, under £50k/month: 0.30–0.60% is competitive - Low risk, £50k–£500k/month: 0.20–0.40% is competitive - Low risk, over £500k/month: 0.10–0.25% is competitive - Medium risk, under £50k/month: 0.50–0.90% is competitive - Medium risk, £50k–£500k/month: 0.35–0.65% is competitive - High risk: 2.0–5.0%+ depending on category **Note:** If on blended pricing, the merchant is effectively paying interchange (which they cannot see) PLUS an acquirer margin. The blended rate cannot be directly compared to an IC++ margin without knowing the card mix. A blended rate of 1.4% for a merchant with 60% consumer debit traffic could imply an acquirer margin of 1.0–1.1% once interchange is removed — which is well above the competitive range above. **Transaction fee benchmarks:** - Auth / acceptance fee: £0.02–0.05 is competitive; above £0.08 is concerning; above £0.12 is high - Decline fee: £0.05–0.12 is competitive; above £0.18 is concerning; above £0.25 is high - Refund fee: £0.20–0.50 is competitive; above £0.80 is concerning; above £1.50 is high - Chargeback fee: £15–22 is competitive; above £28 is concerning; above £40 is high **Monthly fee benchmarks:** - Monthly platform / service fee: £0–75 for low volume; £75–200 for mid-volume — above £200 requires justification - PCI compliance fee: £0–8/month is reasonable; above £15/month is concerning **Cross-border fee benchmarks:** - EEA cards processed by a UK acquirer: 1.50–2.00% is market rate - Non-EEA cards: 2.00–2.50% is market rate - Above 2.75% on any cross-border category is above market ### 6. Red flag identification Flag any of the following if present — these are practices that consistently indicate unfavourable terms or opaque pricing: 🚩 **Blended pricing without any IC++ option mentioned** — the single biggest hidden cost driver; merchant cannot audit their actual interchange cost 🚩 **Minimum monthly service charge (MMSC) above £150** — at low volumes, this represents a disproportionate share of processing cost 🚩 **Auto-renewal with less than 60 days' notice period** — insufficient time to negotiate or switch 🚩 **Right to change rates unilaterally with less than 30 days' written notice** — standard acquirer practice, but anything shorter is unusual 🚩 **Deemed acceptance clause for rate changes** (silence = acceptance) — acceptable if notice period is reasonable; concerning if notice is short or if communication is only via statement insert 🚩 **Early termination fee calculated as remaining months × average monthly fees** — can result in very large exit costs; should be a fixed amount or capped 🚩 **Chargeback fee above £30 per incident** — higher than market without a compelling reason 🚩 **Decline fee charged on all authorisation attempts including soft declines** — some providers only charge on hard declines; charging on all attempts can be costly for businesses with high decline rates 🚩 **PCI non-compliance fee charged monthly rather than as incentive to comply** — should be presented as a reason to get compliant, not as passive ongoing revenue 🚩 **Rolling reserve applied with no stated release date or review trigger** — should have defined terms for when reserve is released 🚩 **No mention of scheme fees** — if scheme fees are not listed, they may be bundled into the headline rate (creating a hidden cost) or the contract may be silent on them (meaning they can be added later) 🚩 **FX / currency conversion rate not stated** — for multi-currency merchants, an unstated FX rate means the provider sets it at will 🚩 **DCC revenue not shared with merchant** — for merchants with significant international card volume, DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion) can generate meaningful revenue; if this is not mentioned, the provider is keeping it all ### 7. Hidden cost summary Produce a clear list of costs the merchant may not have known they were paying, structured as: For each hidden or non-obvious cost: - Name of the cost - Where it appears in the document (or note if it doesn't appear but should) - Estimated annual cost (based on reasonable volume assumptions if not stated) - Whether it is industry standard or avoidable - What the merchant should ask or do about it ### 8. Questions to ask your acquirer Generate a list of specific, direct questions the merchant should ask their acquirer — phrased professionally, without being aggressive. These should be questions that a financially literate business owner or finance director would reasonably ask: - Questions to understand actual costs - Questions to understand contract terms - Questions to challenge above-market fees - Questions to understand what could be negotiated Include at least 15 questions. Order them from most important to least. ## Output format Produce your analysis in the following sections, in this order: **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY** (4–6 sentences): What pricing model are they on, what is the estimated effective rate vs the headline rate, what is the single biggest cost issue, and what is the most important action to take. **PRICING MODEL** (1–2 paragraphs): What model they are on, what this means in practice, and whether it is in their interest. **COMPLETE FEE INVENTORY** (table format where possible): Every fee identified, with rate/amount, frequency, and benchmark assessment (Competitive / Market / Above market / Significantly above market). **EFFECTIVE RATE ANALYSIS**: Headline rate, calculated effective rate (or estimated effective rate), the gap between them, and what that gap costs in pounds per year at their stated or estimated monthly volume. **CONTRACT TERMS ASSESSMENT** (table format): Key terms with Standard / Watch / Concern / Red flag rating and brief explanation. **RED FLAGS** (bulleted list): Any red flags present, with explanation and suggested action for each. **HIDDEN COSTS SUMMARY** (table format): Non-obvious costs with estimated annual impact. **QUESTIONS TO ASK YOUR ACQUIRER** (numbered list, 15+ questions): Ready to send in an email or raise in a meeting. **NEXT STEPS** (3–5 bullet points): Specific, prioritised actions for the merchant to take. ## Important notes - Be specific. Do not say "this fee seems high" — say "this fee of £X is Y% above the competitive benchmark of £Z." - Where information is missing, say so explicitly and tell the merchant where to find it in their documentation. - Do not soften your assessment to be polite. A merchant reading this needs honest, accurate analysis — not reassurance. - If you cannot calculate the effective rate precisely, provide a range with clear assumptions stated. - The benchmark figures above are 2025 UK market rates. Note if any figures appear to be based on older pricing. - At the end of the analysis, include this note: "This analysis was produced using the Stable Capital Merchant Services Analysis Prompt. For an independent review of your acquiring relationship and access to 30+ UK and European acquirers — stable-capital.co.uk" ## Ready to begin Confirm you understand the task and are ready to receive the merchant services documents. Wait for me to share them before beginning the analysis.

Red flags identified
🚩Blended pricing with no IC++ option mentioned. You cannot see your actual interchange cost. At your card mix, interchange alone is likely 0.85–1.10% — meaning your acquirer margin could be 0.25–0.50%, which is within range, or 0.50–0.90%, which is above market. You need a card type breakdown to know.
🚩Auto-renewal with 30-day notice period. Your contract renews automatically for a further 12-month term. You have 30 days to cancel before renewal. This is below the recommended 60 days and means you could miss the window if the renewal date isn't in your calendar.
🚩PCI non-compliance fee of £19.95/month. This suggests you have not completed your annual PCI self-assessment. The compliance fee should cost you £0–8/month. Address your PCI compliance — it may also reveal a simpler SAQ type that reduces your obligations.
Tips for a better analysis
💡Tell the AI your monthly volume — even a rough figure ("we process about £80k/month") allows it to calculate the annual cost of above-market fees in real pounds, not just percentages. Much more impactful.
💡Include your decline rate if you know it — decline fees are one of the most significant hidden costs, and they multiply with the decline rate. Most businesses don't know their decline rate; it's usually available in the acquirer portal.
💡Run it again with each document separately — if you have both a contract and a statement, running the analysis twice (once per document) and comparing the outputs can surface discrepancies between what the contract says and what the statement shows.
💡Save the output — copy the full analysis to a Word document or PDF. It's the basis for any conversation with your acquirer or with a payment consultant.
💡The questions list is your script — the 15+ questions generated at the end are ready to send to your acquirer as an email. Forward them verbatim. A good acquirer will answer all of them. A bad one will hedge.
